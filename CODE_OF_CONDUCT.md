# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We are building WFL to make programming more readable, approachable, and
humane. We pledge to make participation in the WebFirst Language project a
harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible
or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and
expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality,
personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and
orientation.

We also pledge not to discriminate against people for using **AI tools** as
part of their work. WFL itself was built with AI assistance. See
[AI_POLICY.md](AI_POLICY.md).

## Our standards

### Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment

- Being kind, patient, and constructive — especially with beginners  
- Focusing on what is best for the language, users, and long-term maintainability  
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback  
- Assuming good intent; asking clarifying questions before escalating  
- Crediting others’ work and respecting license and attribution requirements  
- Keeping security reports private until a fix is coordinated ([SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md))  
- Writing clear issues and PRs so others can help  

### Examples of unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind  
- Sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention  
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks  
- Public or private harassment  
- Publishing others’ private information without explicit permission  
- Dismissing or attacking someone **because** their contribution involved AI
  tools (see [AI_POLICY.md](AI_POLICY.md))  
- Knowingly submitting malware, credential theft, or supply-chain sabotage  
- Sustained disruption of discussions, issues, or review processes  
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional open-source setting  

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including:

- The GitHub organization, repositories, issues, PRs, discussions, and wikis  
- Official project communication channels (email listed for the project, etc.)  
- Official events or meetings when representing the project  

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in
public spaces.

## Enforcement responsibilities

Project Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of
Conduct. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits,
issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code, and will
communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct in good faith may
face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by other Maintainers
or the copyright holder (Logbie LLC).

## Reporting

Report unacceptable behavior to:

- **Email:** info@logbie.com (subject line recommended: `WFL Code of Conduct`)  
- **Security-related abuse** (e.g. active exploitation): prefer
  [GitHub Security Advisories](https://github.com/WebFirstLanguage/wfl/security/advisories/new)
  or the channels in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md)  

Include as much detail as you can (links, screenshots, dates, what happened).
Reports will be handled as confidentially as practical. Retaliation against
good-faith reporters is itself a Code of Conduct violation.

## Enforcement guidelines

Maintainers will use judgment proportional to the impact:

| Level | Example | Possible consequence |
|---|---|---|
| **1 — Correction** | Inappropriate language or unprofessional tone | Private warning; request to edit or delete |
| **2 — Warning** | Single clear violation or repeated minor issues | Public or private warning; temporary interaction limits |
| **3 — Temporary ban** | Serious or sustained violation | Temporary ban from project spaces |
| **4 — Permanent ban** | Pattern of abuse, harassment, or sabotage | Permanent ban; possible removal of Collaborator access |

These levels are guidance, not a rigid ladder. Severe cases (threats, doxxing,
malware) may jump to temporary or permanent ban immediately.

## Technical disagreement is not misconduct

Strong technical disagreement, blunt code review, and insistence on project
policies (tests, docs, backward compatibility, the No-Unlearning Invariant) are
**not** Code of Conduct violations when delivered without personal attacks or
harassment. Maintainers may still moderate volume or tone if it becomes
disruptive.
